FAQ:


Will I become rich trading stocks?

Maybe or maybe not, that's part of the fun. Trading stocks can be like gambling but with more educated decisions on which stocks you choose, it becomes less like gambling. It is very possible to make trading stocks on a daily basis as your day job. Many people make enough money off of the trades that they execute, that allows them to make it their day job.

Many retirement plans use investments in the stock market as a way to continue getting paid even when you are retired and no longer working. There are many ways to invest your money into the stock market that result in you getting significant returns or allowing you to augment your income.

How do I pick stocks?

Ask 100 people this question and you'll likely get a lot of different answers. Here's what we suggest, pick stocks from companies in areas that intreset you. For example, if you're into technology, specifically Apple, invest in Apple. It makes researching companies more interesting and allows you to stay ontop of what's going on in the industry and market.

There are many ways to pick stocks, but it ultimately comes down to a criteria that is put forth by yourself. Market indicators also exist that make it easier to pick stocks and can give an idea of how a stock's price will move in the near future. These indicators are usually referred to as fundamentals and there are many of them, each with a different meaning and use.

  • Beta: It is the volatility of a stock's price compared to an index (usually the S&P 500)
  • P/E Ratio: It is the relative valuation that is a comparison to a company's peers. The higher the ratio, the more expensive and lower the value of the company compared to its peers.
  • Market Cap: It is the number of shares outstanding multiplied by the current price of the stock. It is used as a quick means to determine the overall value of a company. It shows how much money is invested into the company.
  • Shares Outstanding: It is the current number of shares that are publicly avaible for purchase or are being actively traded. It a general indicator of the liquidability of a company.
  • Average Volume: This indicates the average amount of shares traded over period of time, usually 10 days. It is a good indicator of the liquidability of the company and how popular it is.

What is a dividend?

A dividend is a portion of a company's profits to its stockholders, those who own stocks for that comapny. A dividend is often decided by the board of directors for that comapny. Simple put, the company gives money to its stockholders as a way of keeping investors.Stocks that pay dividends are good stocks to purchase and to "baghold", to hold for a long time. If you hold enough stocks that pay dividends, it can turn into getting a paycheck regulary, without having to work for it.

What is diversification?

Diversification is the process in which one diversifies their portfolio to mitigate risk. In the case of economic events that cause a stock's price to decrease, a person does not lose a large amount of money. By diversifying one's investments into different companies, indices, and markets, the overall risk to losing large amounts of money is decreased. Diversifying one's portfolio also allows the portfolio to remain liquidable during times in which some investments are not liquidated easily.
